Mohammad Ali Jinnah

Founder of Pakistan

Timeline of Jinnah:

  • December 25th, 1876- Born in Wazir Mansion, Karachi
  • 1913- After completing his education, Jinnah joined the Muslim League, a political party that was meant for the better welfare of muslims
  • 1920- Indian National Congress launched a movement of non-cooperation to boycott all aspects of British rule. Jinnah opposed this policy and resigned from the congress. There were by now profound differences between the congress and the Muslim League.
  • 1937- After provincial elections in 1937, the congress refused to form coalition administrations with the Muslim League in mixed areas. Relations between Hindus and Muslims began to deteriorate. In 1940, at a Muslim League session in Lahore, the first official demand was made for the partition of India and the creation of a Muslim state of Pakistan. Jinnah had always believed that Hindu-Muslim unity was possible, but reluctantly came to the view that partition was necessary to safeguard the rights of Indian Muslims.
  • August 14th, 1947- His insistence on this issue through negotiations with the British government resulted in the partition of India and the formation of the state of Pakistan on 14 August 1947. This occurred against a backdrop of widespread violence between Muslims, Hindus and Sikhs, and a vast movement of populations between the new states of Pakistan and India in which hundreds of thousands died.
  • September 11th, 1948- Jinnah became the first governor general of Pakistan, but died of tuberculosis on 11 September 1948.
